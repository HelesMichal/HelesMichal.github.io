NBA Game Predictor

A leakage-safe machine learning pipeline for predicting NBA game outcomes

A pure-Python pipeline that scrapes NBA stats, engineers a leakage-safe feature set, trains a margin-regression model with isotonic-calibrated win probabilities, and predicts upcoming games — with the last 5 days of predictions re-checked against reality every run. Automated end-to-end with GitHub Actions.

What it does

Fetch

Pulls games and team advanced stats from nba_api, plus player advanced stats (BPM/VORP/WS) and current injuries from Basketball-Reference. Regular season and playoffs are tagged separately.

Build features

Assembles rolling team form, Pythagorean expectation, schedule & travel, home-court advantage, season-level advanced stats, star strength, and dual Elo rating books — all leakage-safe via shift(1) before any rolling calculation.

Train

An XGBoost regressor predicts point margin, mapped to win probability via isotonic regression. Trains on the last 12 seasons with exponential per-season sample weights, excluding the 2019-20 COVID-bubble season entirely. Greedy correlation pruning drops redundant features.

Predict

For each scheduled game: pulls the latest feature row per team, applies injury adjustments for players listed Out/Doubtful, and outputs win probabilities plus predicted margin. Re-predicts the last 5 days of completed games to report rolling accuracy.

Feature engineering highlights

Rolling team form

6-game rolling window (not 10) so the model reacts to lineup changes, trades, and hot/cold streaks without lagging real form shifts by weeks.

Elo ratings

Separate books for regular season vs. playoffs, with a 538-style margin-of-victory multiplier, home bonus, and season-to-season regression toward the mean.

Star strength

Per-team-season score derived from each player's minutes, usage rate, BPM, VORP, and win shares — captures the impact of missing stars beyond simple win/loss stats.

Schedule & travel

Rest days, back-to-backs, travel miles, timezone shifts, and high-altitude games (Denver, Utah) — the physical toll behind the box score.

Automated end-to-end

Two GitHub Actions workflows keep the pipeline running without a server: a daily prediction job that sends today's games plus the last-5-days accuracy to a Telegram bot, and a weekly retrain job that refreshes the model every Monday. Both run entirely on GitHub's free tier — no infrastructure to maintain.
